PRISMPolynomial Representations forInteraction-Structured Motor Control

PRISM makes interaction terms among joints, velocities, contacts, and load learnable from deployable proprioceptive observations.

Seung Hyun Lee and Stella X. Yu

Motivation. Robot behavior is shaped by coupled body-state effects: a joint angle matters differently under changing velocity, contact, load, and commanded motion.

Deployable setup. PRISM uses only the policy's proprioceptive stream and adds no tactile input, wrench sensing, contact labels, or simulator-only physical parameters.

Body momentum
joint angle + velocity → motion-dependent load
Contact onset
gripper / joint slowdown → object interaction cue
Slip or drag
command vs. realized motion → dynamics mismatch

PRISM makes physical state couplings learnable

A compact degree-2 conditioner over deployable proprioception.

PRISM gives the policy learnable products of deployable proprioceptive signals, so coupled physical effects can be represented directly.

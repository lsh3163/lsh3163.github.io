PRISMPolynomial Representations for Interaction-Structured Motor Control

PRISM makes interaction terms among joints, velocities, contacts, and load learnable from deployable proprioceptive observations.

Seung Hyun Lee and Stella X. Yu

Paper PDF Code coming soon BFM Results LIBERO Results

Motivation. Robot behavior is shaped by coupled body-state effects: a joint angle matters differently under changing velocity, contact, load, and commanded motion.

Deployable setup. PRISM uses only the policy's proprioceptive stream and adds no tactile input, wrench sensing, contact labels, or simulator-only physical parameters.

PRISM learns proprioceptive interactions, not a new controller

A compact degree-2 conditioner over robot state history.

The observation stream, action space, controller, and training recipe stay fixed; only the proprioceptive conditioner changes.

Deployable state

State history, previous actions, and commands are the only inputs.

No tactile, force, wrench, contact-label, or privileged simulator parameter is added.
